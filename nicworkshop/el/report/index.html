NICVE Workshop Analysis

Analysis of participant responses from the Nicosia International Airport Virtual Environment workshop

Workshop Booth Play Activity Overview:

  • 22 participants in groups of 5-6
  • 15-minute semi-guided interactions with the virtual environment
  • Majority of participants aged 55+
  • Mix of technical experience levels
  • Strong personal connections to the airport's heritage

1. Demographic Analysis

View Analysis

Analysis of participant backgrounds, including age distribution, tech experience, and connection to the airport's heritage.

Key Finding: Predominantly 55+ age group with balanced gender distribution and strong personal/family connections to the airport.

2. Booth Play Experience

View Analysis

Analysis of Likert-scale responses about the virtual environment experience, including usability, content clarity, and immersion.

Key Finding: High usability scores across all tech experience levels, with strong correlation between content clarity and immersion.

3. VE Improvement Priorities

View Analysis

Analysis of how participants ranked different potential improvements to the virtual environment.

Key Finding: Strong preference for historical media documentation and 3D visualization over narrative and community features.

4. Tech Comfort Impact Analysis

View Analysis

Detailed analysis of how technology experience levels influenced participant engagement and preferences.

Key Finding: Tech-experienced users reported higher immersion despite being less concerned about visual realism.

5. Proximity to Airport

View Analysis

Analysis of how direct experience with the airport (primary vs secondary connections) influenced experience and priorities around the VE.

Key Finding: Direct experience led to greater interest in interactive features and less concern about visual realism, while those with secondary connections emphasized documentation and visual authenticity.
